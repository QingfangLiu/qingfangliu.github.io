Hi, I'm Qingfang

I’m a research fellow at the Learning and Decision-Making Unit at the National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA). I earned my Ph.D. in Psychology from The Ohio State University, where I specialized in cognitive and computational neuroscience in the Model-based Cognitive Neuroscience Lab.

My work bridges cognitive science, neuroscience, and computational modeling to understand how people learn, make decisions, and — more broadly — how intelligence emerges in the human brain.

I’m now exploring data science and AI opportunities in industry, where I aim to apply my analytical rigor to real-world challenges.
